# Final synthesis

Combine the extract, analysis, and critique into a decision-ready brief.

## Required structure

1. **Bottom line (1 paragraph)** — The single most important takeaway. If the reader only reads this, what do they need to know?

2. **Key findings (3–5 bullets)** — Each bullet cites which step the evidence comes from so the reader can trace the claim back:
   - Strong findings (validated by the critique)
   - Findings that need caveats (flagged by the critique)

3. **Open questions** — Things the source material did not resolve and a downstream investigator should pursue.

4. **Recommendation** — One concrete next step.

## Tone

- No filler or executive-speak.
- Lead with the conclusion in every paragraph.
- If the critique flagged a claim as weak, do not repeat it uncritically — either drop it or mark it explicitly as "unverified."

Keep the entire brief under 500 words.
